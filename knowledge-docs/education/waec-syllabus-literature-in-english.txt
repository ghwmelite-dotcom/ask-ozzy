EXAMINATION STRUCTURE

The Literature in English examination consists of three papers:

Paper 1: Objective Test
- Duration: 1 hour
- Marks: 50
- Format: 50 multiple-choice questions
- Content: General literary knowledge, unseen prose and poetry passages, and context questions on the prescribed Shakespearean text
- Unseen prose passages: 120-150 words
- No essay questions on the Shakespearean text

Paper 2: Prose (Essay)
- Duration: 1 hour 15 minutes
- Marks: 50
- Section A: African Prose — candidates answer one essay question
- Section B: Non-African Prose — candidates answer one essay question

Paper 3: Drama and Poetry (Essay)
- Duration: 2 hours 30 minutes
- Marks: 100
- Section A: African Drama — answer one question
- Section B: Non-African Drama — answer one question
- Section C: African Poetry — answer one question
- Section D: Non-African Poetry — answer one question

AIMS AND OBJECTIVES

The examination tests candidates' ability to:

1. Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of the content of prescribed literary texts
2. Appreciate literary techniques and devices used by authors
3. Relate themes and issues in literary works to real-life situations
4. Analyze character, plot, setting, theme, and style in literary works
5. Demonstrate knowledge of general literary terms and concepts
6. Interpret unseen prose and poetry passages
7. Write coherent, well-organized literary essays
8. Show understanding of the socio-cultural contexts of literary works

GENERAL LITERARY KNOWLEDGE (Paper 1)

Topics covered in the objective test include:

Literary Terms and Devices:
- Figures of speech (metaphor, simile, personification, hyperbole, irony, etc.)
- Literary devices (alliteration, assonance, onomatopoeia, rhyme, rhythm)
- Narrative techniques (point of view, flashback, foreshadowing, stream of consciousness)
- Dramatic devices (soliloquy, aside, dramatic irony, comic relief)
- Poetic forms (sonnet, ode, ballad, elegy, epic, lyric)

Literary Concepts:
- Genre (prose, drama, poetry)
- Theme, plot, setting, characterization
- Conflict (internal, external)
- Mood and tone
- Symbolism and imagery
- Satire, allegory, and parody

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

KEY NOTES FOR CANDIDATES

1. Context questions focus on theme, characterization, style, and setting
2. Unseen prose passages are limited to 120-150 words
3. No essay question shall be set on the Shakespearean text (context questions only)
4. Candidates should read all prescribed texts thoroughly
5. Essays should demonstrate critical analysis, not mere narration of plot
6. Candidates should be familiar with the socio-cultural backgrounds of the texts
